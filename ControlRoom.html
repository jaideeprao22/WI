Outbreak escalation — R₀ estimate → decision

Method: r = ln 2 ÷ doubling time; R₀ ≈ 1 + r × serial interval. This is an estimate from the figures you enter — not a measured value. R₀ is one input only; an emergency decision also weighs case severity, hospital load and official lab confirmation.

Resource lead-time — “Days Until Critical”

A warning threshold buys time. Enter a resource (e.g. emergency beds) and how fast it is filling — the room tells you how many days until it hits critical, so you act in business hours, not at 3 AM.

Lead time assumes the current rate holds — a planning estimate, not a guarantee. Default: 500 beds, 350 full, +20/day → ~5 days to critical.

Alert tiers — warning vs critical REFERENCE

TierMeaningRespond inChannel
NORMALBaseline, within expected range—Passive logging
WARNINGDegrading trend, not yet a crisisHours–daysDashboard, email
CRITICALThreshold breached, crisisMinutesSMS, paging, RRT

Separating warning from critical prevents alert fatigue — the system stays quiet until a trend is real, then escalates hard.
